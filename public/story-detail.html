Sarah's Design Studio: From Side-Hustle to Six Figures

Sarah Johnson
April 15, 2025

The Beginning

Three years ago, I was working a 9-to-5 job as a graphic designer at a marketing agency. While I enjoyed the work, I always felt constrained by client requirements and corporate guidelines. I started taking on small freelance projects on the weekends â€“ creating logos, business cards, and simple websites for friends and their connections.

What began as a creative outlet quickly grew into something more. Within six months, I was earning nearly as much from my weekend projects as I was from my full-time job. That's when I realized there might be an opportunity to turn my side-hustle into something more substantial.

The Turning Point

The decision to leave my stable job wasn't easy. I had benefits, a reliable paycheck, and the security that comes with traditional employment. But I also had a growing client list, a portfolio of work I was proud of, and a vision for the kind of design studio I wanted to create.

I decided to take the leap, but not without preparation. I spent three months building up my emergency fund, creating systems for my business, and developing a clear service offering. When I finally submitted my resignation, I already had enough client work lined up to replace 70% of my income.

"The scariest part wasn't leaving my job â€“ it was realizing that every aspect of my business success now rested entirely on my shoulders."

Building a Sustainable Business

The first year as a full-time solopreneur was a rollercoaster. I made mistakes â€“ undercharging for projects, overcommitting to deadlines, and trying to be everything to everyone. I quickly learned that running a successful solo business isn't just about being good at your craft; it's about being strategic with your time, energy, and focus.

I started implementing changes that transformed my business:

  • Specialization: Instead of being a generalist designer, I focused specifically on brand identity systems for wellness and lifestyle businesses.
  • Value-based pricing: I stopped charging by the hour and created packages based on the value I provided to clients.
  • Systems and automation: I developed templates, workflows, and automated processes that reduced administrative time by 60%.
  • Client selection: I became more selective about the clients I worked with, focusing on long-term relationships rather than one-off projects.

Reaching Six Figures

By the end of my second year, I had hit a significant milestone: $100,000 in annual revenue. What made this achievement particularly meaningful was that I accomplished it while working fewer hours than I had at my agency job.

The key wasn't working harder â€“ it was working smarter. By focusing on high-value clients, creating efficient systems, and leveraging my specialized expertise, I was able to increase my income while improving my quality of life.

Lessons Learned

Looking back on my journey from employee to six-figure solopreneur, there are several key lessons that stand out:

  1. Start before you're ready. If I had waited until I felt completely prepared to launch my business, I never would have started.
  2. Invest in yourself. The courses, coaching, and tools I invested in paid for themselves many times over.
  3. Build a support network. Finding other solopreneurs to share experiences with has been invaluable for both practical advice and emotional support.
  4. Focus on systems, not just skills. Being excellent at your craft is just the beginning â€“ creating systems that support your business is what allows you to scale.
  5. Know your worth. Charging what you're truly worth isn't just good for your bank account â€“ it's essential for providing the level of service your clients deserve.

What's Next

As I look to the future, I'm excited about the possibilities ahead. I'm currently developing a digital course to share my branding methodology with other designers, and I'm exploring ways to create more passive income streams.

What I love most about being a solopreneur is the freedom to evolve my business as I evolve as a person. My business today looks very different from what I imagined when I started, and I expect it will continue to transform in the years ahead.
